Introduction

Wanderwise is a custom travel-planning assistant (a purpose-built GPT) that I designed and built. It helps travelers discover the most popular points of interest in any city and turns them into a practical, well-paced, day-by-day itinerary tailored to their interests, time, and travel style. The idea grew out of my own trip planning: the hardest part is rarely finding information; it is filtering the flood of websites, blogs, and videos into a plan you can actually follow.

Objective

The objective was to design a conversational AI assistant that gathers and filters attraction information for a destination and produces a realistic, personalized itinerary, while understanding natural descriptions of travel style such as "relaxed," "not packed," or "just the highlights."

Project Overview

Wanderwise runs entirely through conversation. It greets the user, asks where they want to go, and then gathers just two to four preferences such as trip length, interests, and pace. Before building anything, it presents six to ten genuinely popular attractions so the traveler can choose what matters most to them.

From those choices, Wanderwise builds a day-by-day itinerary that groups nearby stops, respects opening hours, and includes meal breaks, rest stops, and realistic travel times between locations. The plan adapts to the requested pace, whether that is a relaxed trip with a few highlights or a packed schedule that covers as much as possible.

Process

This was an individual project; I was the sole designer and builder. I worked through the following steps:

  • Framed the problem as filtering information rather than finding it, and defined the core user experience.
  • Designed the conversation flow: ask for a destination first, then gather just two to four preferences.
  • Wrote and iterated on the GPT's instructions, including how it ranks attractions, paces days, and formats output.
  • Tested with sample destinations and refined the prompts, presentation, and guardrails based on the results.

Tools & Technologies

  • OpenAI Custom GPT builder (GPT-5.5 Instant)
  • Prompt engineering and instruction design
  • Conversational UX and interaction design
  • Iterative testing with real destinations

Value Proposition

Wanderwise showcases prompt and instruction design, conversational UX thinking, and the ability to translate an ambiguous everyday problem into a structured, usable AI product, complete with sensible defaults and guardrails against unreliable output.

It aligns with my personal value proposition of turning ambiguous problems into reliable products and applying AI responsibly. For those in software, data, and AI roles, it demonstrates end-to-end product design rather than a single feature.

Key Learnings

The biggest lesson was balancing personalization with simplicity: asking too many questions slowed the experience, while too few produced generic itineraries. I learned to collect only the most important information and infer sensible defaults whenever possible.

Testing also reshaped the product. Early feedback showed users wanted to choose their own attractions rather than receive a fixed schedule, so I reworked the flow to present a ranked list and let them select before the itinerary is generated. I moved images inline with each attraction to make the list easier to scan, removed rigid step titles for a friendlier tone, and added guardrails so the assistant never invents attractions, prices, or hours.
